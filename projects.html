Sunflower NLP-C

A product of PIXNet hackathon Taiwan. I utilized the Stanford Chinese Segmenter to tokenize PIXNet blog data from their API. I grouped words and phrases, with the hope of quantifying political beliefs in the Taiwanese blogosphere. My development stack was a Jython NLP to a mongoDB backend, which was to be curated and pushed to an AJAX-style database.

Undergraduate Thesis

Under the supervision of Prof. Ken Jackson, I investigated numerically solving stochastic differential equations. I looked into finite difference and finite element methods on the Black-Scholes equation for multi-factor bonds. With an eye on processor limits, I also implemented various adaptive meshing methods to save processing time and memory.

TimeTracks

A product of AngelHacks NYC, 2013. Myself and 4 other friends sought to fill a need amongst construction contractors. We built an Android app to leverage the GPS capabilities on a smartphone. Much like Google Fit now, it would track a user's location over the course of the work day automatically generate a timesheet at the end of the week; it would only require location tagging from the user. I was responsible for the Google Maps UI, and for aggregating locations.
